Know the Ethiopian Orthodox Church

One of the oldest Christian churches in the world, with a rich heritage stretching back nearly two thousand years

A Living Ancient Tradition

The Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church is one of the few pre-colonial Christian churches in Africa and one of the earliest in the world. Founded in the 4th century, it has preserved ancient traditions, liturgical practices, and sacred texts that have remained virtually unchanged for over 1,600 years.

The word "Tewahedo" means "unified" — reflecting the church's unique theological understanding. The church maintains its own distinct canon of scripture, including the Book of Enoch and the Book of Jubilees, which are not found in most other Christian traditions.

Our worship is rich with sacred music, elaborate vestments, and ancient hymns composed by Saint Yared in the 6th century — a tradition of liturgical chant that predates Gregorian chant. The church celebrates a vibrant calendar of fasts and feasts that mark the spiritual rhythm of daily life.
